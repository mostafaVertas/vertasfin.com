The 3 Conversations Every Modern CFO Wants — and What to Stop Reporting

Learn why less reporting can drive more strategic action. Discover the 3 conversations that replace overwhelming dashboards with insight-led decisions.

We live in a world where dashboards are overflowing, KPIs are everywhere, and decision fatigue is real. But when we sit down with forward-thinking CFOs, they tell us a different story: “I don’t want more reports. I want fewer, deeper conversations.”

The Problem with Traditional Performance Reviews

Typical monthly performance reviews focus on slides, charts, and variances. But by the time the review ends, executives are overwhelmed — and actionable insight is lost in the noise.

Modern finance leaders are shifting toward a new model: one focused on dialogue over data dumps.

The 3 Conversations Every CFO Really Wants

1. Why Are We Off Plan?

Instead of rehashing every budget line, focus on what materially changed — and why. Was it customer behavior? Pricing? A one-time operational miss?

2. What Can We Do Now?

Strategic finance means being agile. Highlight options to course-correct. “Do we cut spend? Adjust goals? Reallocate resources?” This is where value is created.

3. Where Are We Headed Next?

End every review with a forward view. Are we trending toward recovery or further risk? Provide scenarios and updated forecasts — not just YTD actuals.

Stop Showing These in Leadership Reviews

  • Every variance over $1,000
  • Month-over-month bar charts with no insight
  • Ten-slide decks of metrics with no decisions

Instead, bring one page with key messages, drivers, and your strategic POV.
